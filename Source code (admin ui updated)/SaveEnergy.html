SAVE ENERGY TIPS

Energy is essential for powering our homes, but its changing cost often affect our budget more than anticipated. It may seem difficult at times to control our energy consumption, but there are simple acts that we can incorporate into our daily lives that can have an impact in minimizing consumption. Some of these may sound cliche, but it is important not to take them for granted.

The following are some ways to help you lower your energy use, save money, and contribute to the environment:

  • Unplug your electronics when not in use: Did you know? Even when turned off, electronics still consume energy if they are plugged in. Unplugging them can help in saving energy.
  • Turn off the lights when not in use: This simple act when put in a regular practice can help in saving electricity and reducing the overall consumption of your household.
  • Use natural light during the day: Utilize sunlight during the day by opening up curtains and blinds to reduce the need for artificial lighting.
  • Regularly clean your appliances: Cleaning your appliances can help saving energy, as dust and dirt can build up on appliances over time which caused them to work harder than necessary.
  • Invest in energy-efficient appliances: These appliances are designed to operate using minimal energy, which not only helps reduce our electricity bills but also minimizes our carbon footprint on the environment.
  • Use LED light bulbs over fluorescent and incandescent bulb: Did you know? LED bulbs use less energy and last longer than traditional light bulbs.
  • Make use of your computer's energy saving-feature: Take advantage of your computer's energy-saving features by setting it to sleep or hibernate mode when not in use.
  • Do not open your fridge longer than necessary: Opening the fridge longer causes it to consume more energy as it needs to maintain its temperature.
  • Insulate your home: Proper insulation helps regulate temperature inside your home, reducing the need for cooling appliances.
  • Match your aircon horsepower (HP) to the size of your room: Using an air conditioner with a higher HP than necessary wastes energy.
